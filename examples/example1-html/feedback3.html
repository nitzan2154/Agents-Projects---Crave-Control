Vegetable Beef with Rice Soup

Rank 1/3
By Marie | Low Cholesterol ★★★ (1)
Time: 40m
Calories: 382.4 kcal
Servings: 4.0
Carbs: 53.4%
Protein: 27.9%
Fat: 18.7%

Ingredients

  • ground beef: 1/2
  • rice: 1
  • diced tomatoes: 1/2
  • beef broth: 1
  • water: 2

Instructions

Brown ground beef in oil. Stir frequently to break up large pieces. Drain off fat. Add rice, tomatoes, beef broth and water. Bring to a boil, then cover and lower to simmer for 10 minutes. Add mixed vegetables and cook 10 minutes longer. Add salt and pepper to taste.

Simple Fried Rice

Rank 2/3
By Blain | White Rice ★★★ (2)
Time: 20m
Calories: 448.4 kcal
Servings: 2.0
Carbs: 80.6%
Protein: 10.4%
Fat: 8.9%

Ingredients

  • egg: 1
  • onion: 1
  • garlic powder: 1/4
  • powdered ginger: 1
  • unsalted butter: 1
  • soy sauce: 1

Instructions

Cook your rice however you choose. Put it in a container and let it sit in the refrigerator for a day or two (old rice just works better). Use just a little bit of butter and scramble your egg make sure break it up into small pieces. Set the egg aside for a minute. Put half of the butter in the pan and saute the diced onions. Now add the rice, the scrambled egg, to the pan and put in the rest of the butter. Add soy sauce now, use just enough to taste and colour. I don't think I even use .5 tbsp as I don't like it too salty. Sprinkle on your ginger and garlic powder. Keep stirring and breaking up any chunks of rice that are stuck together. Continue heating the rice for about 2 minutes. Eat immediately and enjoy!

Tilapia and Ginger-Sesame Roasted Vegetables

Rank 3/3
By cathianne | One Dish Meal ★★★★★ (3)
Time: 1h
Calories: 488.1 kcal
Servings: 2.0
Carbs: 49.6%
Protein: 31.0%
Fat: 19.5%

Ingredients

  • yellow bell pepper: 2
  • red onion: 3
  • olive oil: 1
  • lime juice: 1/2
  • red pepper flakes: 2
  • salt: 1

Instructions

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Combine olive oil, sesame oil, lime juice, pepper flakes, salt and ginger. Place potatoes, pepper, and onion in a pyrex-type dish. The dish should be just long enough to fit both fillets and about 2 inches deep. Place the vegetables in the baking dish and toss with the oil-spice sauce. Bake for 25 minutes, stirring once. Remove from oven and push the vegetables to one side of the cooking dish. Place first fillet in dish and cover with the vegetables. Push vegetables aside to place other fillet in dish and cover it with the remaining vegetables. Bake for another 20 minutes or until tilapia is thoroughly cooked. It should flake and still be very moist.
